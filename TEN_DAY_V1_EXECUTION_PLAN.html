Aurora Galactica: 10-Day V1 Execution Plan

A practical plan to ship v1, reduce launch risk, and land the highest-value architectural seams without stopping for a full platform refactor before release.

Date: March 26, 2026 Project: Aurora Galactica Repo: /Users/stevenwoods/Documents/Codex-Test1

Purpose

This plan is designed to do four things at once:

  1. finish the current v1 launch push
  2. reduce launch risk materially
  3. strengthen the regression and release pipeline
  4. add enough architectural seams that the codebase remains evolvable after launch
Key decision: the next 10 days should target launch + thin-seam refactor, not a full generalized arcade platform.

Success Definition

  • Aurora Galactica is ready to launch as v1, or is one very small final decision away from promotion.
  • The launch blocker list is short, stable, and justified.
  • The beta-review cluster is largely closed or downgraded to low-risk polish.
  • Stage 2 and Stage 4 fairness have better tuning and stronger evidence than today.
  • Release-readiness materials are complete: security review, code docs, consistent public docs, and a short players guide.
  • The runtime has thin architectural seams: gameDef, data-driven tuning, optional-system boundaries, and stable telemetry vocabulary.

Scope

In Scope

  • Launch blockers and beta-review closure
  • Stage 2 and Stage 4 fairness tuning
  • Security review and release-readiness pass
  • Players guide and documentation refresh
  • Production vs non-production data-path separation
  • Thin-seam refactor work that helps Aurora immediately

Out of Scope

  • Full arcade-platform extraction
  • Implementing Galaxian as a second game
  • Generalized multi-game runtime/menu
  • Large speculative framework work
  • Theme/template expansion unrelated to launch

Recommended team size: 7 to 8 active agents plus one coordinating lead.

Core Principles

  1. No broad refactor without a regression guard.
  2. No speculative platform work that does not help Aurora today.
  3. Every gameplay change needs a manual reason, a harness bar, and a rollback path.
  4. Refactor work must be seam-oriented, not aesthetic.
  5. If the plan slips, cut platform ambition before cutting launch integrity.

Risks And Mitigations

Risk Why it matters Mitigation
Stage 2 / Stage 4 tuning takes longer than expected These remain the most meaningful gameplay blockers. Dedicated owners, persona/pressure metrics, and quick reverts when a pass clearly regresses.
Thin-seam refactor creates late regressions Structural churn can silently change gameplay. Only land seam-oriented work behind harness guards and preserve rollback paths.
Harness flakiness corrupts decision-making Good changes get blocked by noisy tests. Dedicated harness ownership, cleaner timeouts, quarantine known-flaky paths.
Documentation and release work gets squeezed That creates sloppy launches even if gameplay is good. Separate release/docs track with work starting before Day 10.
Scope creep toward full platformization That risks missing both launch and architecture goals. Hold the line on thin seams only; defer full engine extraction and second-game work.

Decision Rules If We Slip

Cut First

  1. Broad architectural ambition
  2. Non-essential UI polish
  3. Lower-priority post-v1 documentation polish

Do Not Cut

  1. Launch integrity
  2. Stage 2 and Stage 4 fairness work
  3. Critical mechanic regression coverage
  4. Security and release-readiness review

Thin-Seam Investments To Keep

  • gameDef
  • Stage-tuning data tables
  • Explicit optional systems
  • Mechanic-level harness abstractions
  • Stable telemetry vocabulary

These are the highest-value incremental investments because they improve Aurora today and directly reduce the cost of post-v1 platformization.

Final Recommendation

The best achievable 10-day plan is:

  1. launch Aurora Galactica v1
  2. strengthen the harness and release workflow
  3. land thin seams that keep the project evolvable
  4. defer full platform extraction until immediately after release

That gets the product out the door without trapping the codebase in a non-evolvable state.
